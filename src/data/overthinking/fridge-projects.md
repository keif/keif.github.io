I think we need a new term to describe weekend projects. Yeah, including vibe-coded ones.

"Fridge Projects."

When you were a kid, your good grades and artwork would get stuck on the fridge. That's the equivalent to a LOT of these vibe-coded projects — they're good starts, but you have a lot to learn! A little pat on the head and positive reinforcement for our lil' coders out there. You'll make it one day!

No one mistakes fridge art for something hanging in the Louvre (I mean, unless you're Banksy, or you duct tape a banana to the wall).

AI has lowered the bar of entry, to the point that some (supposedly experienced, educated) developers think using AI blindly is "good" because they "have a degree, it's fine, I know what to look for."

These developers never seem to understand that "someone vibe-coding without experience" does not, in fact, know what to look for.

But by lowering the bar for creation, we are also raising the bar for expectations. Anyone can make a stick figure, and a lot of the apps being created today are just the stick figure. You may add some color, or some additional flair. But in a pile of papers, no one can tell the difference.

Nobody is impressed.

Building software used to be the achievement. Nowadays? Usefulness is the achievement — making something that people WANT to use, and REMEMBER to use.

I use AI. I trust it as much as I trust the people I work with — which means I REVIEW THE WORK. I'll use AI as well to review, because it can be faster than me, and it can highlight exact files and lines of concern for review in a changeset. It's helpful!

For example, I use [Plex](https://www.plex.tv/) for my home media server. I wanted to gather some music videos to play, and random clips online, in general. I found "Downr" — dev support was basically "do not bother replying." Not exactly a product I wanted to put money into.

So I wrote my own implementation. Not a Chrome plugin (which felt sketchy in and of itself) — but an actual implementation — locally. Not on a server with ads, or shoe-horned "10 videos free! Additional videos as part of a monthly subscription!"

But it's good enough for the fridge. Good job, champ! You made something!

That's the [YouTube Playlist Generator](/posts/headless-youtube-playlist-generator/). The [YouTube Downloader](https://github.com/keif/ytdl) (which kind of inspired this post). The [image compression site](/posts/image-optimizer/) (hosted on [Hetzner](https://www.hetzner.com/), and runnable locally!). The random utility app with three users. The side project that solves one very specific problem for one very specific user.

There's nothing wrong with that.

Not every drawing belongs in the Louvre.

That doesn't mean it doesn't belong on the fridge.

Can fridge-worthy work end up running a business? Being a subscription? Of course! How many famous artists started out hanging their work on the fridge before they got found/published? It's just more rare (and could depend on any number of things — a good network, crossing the right person at the right time, a "small loan" of millions of dollars from your wealthy relatives).
